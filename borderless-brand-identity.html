Visual Identity System — v1.0

Built on two blues
and a borderless idea.

A dollar-native, dual-rail money brand. Confident, modern, and trustworthy — the identity is engineered around the cobalt and deep-navy blues that already live in the Borderless mark.

01

The mark

The four-blade pinwheel reads as motion across borders — money moving freely. Give it room and keep it in brand blue. A reversed (knockout) version carries it onto dark and photographic surfaces.

Primary — on white / light
Reversed — on Deep Blue 900
✓ Clear space

Keep padding around the logo equal to the height of the mark. Minimum width 96px digital.

✓ Approved colour

Brand blue on light; solid white knockout on dark. Nothing else.

✕ Don't recolour or gradient-fill the wordmark

The mark may use the two blues; the wordmark stays a single blue.

✕ Don't stretch, rotate, add shadows or outlines

No distortion, no effects, no boxing the logo in.

03

Typography

A geometric-humanist system that echoes the rounded, confident lowercase of the wordmark. Headlines carry character; body stays quiet and legible; money is always set in a tabular monospace so columns line up to the cent.

Display / Headlines — Sora
Hold dollars,
spend borderless.
H1 / 48–76Aa Bb Cc 0123
H2 / 28–34Aa Bb Cc 0123
H3 / 20–22Aa Bb Cc 0123
Body / UI — Hanken Grotesk
Your USD account and USDC wallet, side by side, behind one balance. Move between them in a tap, spend on either card, and earn on what sits idle. Clear, plain, and human — no jargon where a normal word will do.
Numerals / Data — IBM Plex Mono (tabular)
$ 12,480.00  ·  +4.00% APY  ·  0x9f…3ad1

Balances, rates, transaction amounts and on-chain hashes — always tabular so digits align in rows.

The official wordmark keeps its existing lettering. The families above are the product & marketing type system around it — swap in a licensed equivalent if brand prefers (e.g. Plus Jakarta Sans for display, IBM Plex Sans for body).

05

Personality & voice

Borderless sounds like a sharp friend who happens to understand global money — direct, plain, present tense, never corporate.

Borderless

Global by default. Dollars and stablecoins as one. Distance and rails are the bank's problem, not the user's.

Clear

Plain language over finance-speak. Say "move money," not "initiate a transfer." Numbers do the talking.

Trustworthy

Calm, precise, traceable. Every figure reconciles; every transaction links to its proof.

On a CTA
✓ "Convert USD to USDC"
✕ "Initiate cross-rail liquidity transfer"
On yield
✓ "Earn 6% on idle USDC. Variable. You hold the risk."
✕ "Maximize your returns with our products!"
